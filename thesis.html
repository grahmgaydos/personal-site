FINDING

After the OMB memos, both action count and total obligation moved up — but spend grew faster than the simple count expansion would predict. +46.5% more contracts, +70.1% more dollars. The intensive margin (per-contract value) moved alongside the extensive margin (frequency), the opposite of what the simplest procurement-as-signalling story would predict. Significance testing and falsification design are reported in the thesis itself.

03 — Question

What does federal AI procurement guidance actually do?

The OMB memoranda M-24-10 (March 2024) and M-24-18 (September 2024) set out detailed expectations for how federal agencies were to acquire, manage, and govern AI systems. The framing is enabling: clarifying procurement pathways, removing friction, setting the bar for responsible adoption.

But what happens in the contract data? Do agencies sign more AI contracts, larger ones, or both? Or does administrative guidance produce signalling without substance — language that changes faster than the spending lines underneath it?

This thesis treats the OMB memos as a quasi-experimental intervention and tests their measurable effect on agency-level AI procurement behaviour, distinguishing between extensive and intensive margins.

04 — Method

Interrupted time series, agency-month panel.

The unit of analysis is the agency-month. AI-related contract actions are identified using a combination of NAICS code filtering, PSC code filtering, and keyword matching against the contract description field — calibrated against a hand-coded validation sample.

Specification
ITS with agency & month-of-year fixed effects (fixest)
Outcome 1
AI contract action count (extensive margin)
Outcome 2
AI obligation $ (intensive × extensive)
Treatment
Bundled OMB intervention indicator (M-24-10 + M-24-18)
Controls
log(total agency procurement); time-varying agency size
Falsification
Placebo timing tests at false intervention dates
Robustness
Alternative AI identifiers; alternative bandwidths

The headline chart visualises the level shifts above the descriptive series. Statistical tests for the joint significance of the treatment indicators, and the falsification design, are developed in the thesis itself.

05 — Implication

What this means for how we read AI policy.

If administrative AI guidance moves both action counts and per-action dollar values, the picture is more interesting than either of the simple stories. Procurement memos are not just coordination signals lowering friction at the margin; they appear to legitimise larger, more ambitious AI procurements that might not previously have cleared institutional review.

That has practical implications for anyone reading federal AI activity from the outside. A surge in AI activity after a guidance memo is not just paperwork — it is also a budget event. The two effects need to be measured together to be understood.

It also has implications for the broader argument that procurement is where governance actually happens. Procurement guidance moves the visible surface of AI activity quickly, and apparently moves real money behind it. Whether the activity is converting into deployed capacity — and whether the spend is concentrated in the right places — is a different question, and one this thesis treats as open.
